Living between cultures, building things that matter

From Beijing to Rotterdam, I've spent 15 years building a life across two worlds. These are the personal projects I care about most — tools for the Chinese community in the Netherlands, family memories, and helping parents explore this beautiful country with their kids.

About Me

Fifteen years ago I left Beijing with a medical degree from Peking University and a one-way ticket to the Netherlands. What followed was a PhD at Erasmus MC in Rotterdam, a career shift into health consulting in the UK, and the slow, sometimes messy, always rewarding work of putting down roots in a country that wasn't mine.

Today I live near Rotterdam with my Dutch husband and our four-year-old son James, who is joyfully growing up between two languages and two cultures. Outside of my work as Chief Scientist at a UK consulting firm, I channel my energy into building things for the communities I belong to.

These personal projects reflect what I care about: helping Chinese families thrive in the Netherlands, preserving the stories that connect generations, and making it easier for parents to discover all the wonderful things this small country has to offer.
